# D&D 5e Downtime Activities

## Crafting
- **Description:** Crafting involves creating magic items, mundane goods, and more.
- **Time Required:** Typically requires several days to weeks depending on the complexity of the item. Some magic items can take months or even years to craft.
- **Cost:** Costs for materials and tools vary widely but are generally significant. Magic item crafting may require rare ingredients and specific spells.

## Training
- **Description:** Training covers learning new languages, tools, and proficiencies.
- **Time Required:** Generally requires several weeks or months of consistent practice to gain a new proficiency.
- **Cost:** Costs for training vary; some schools or guilds may charge fees. Languages can be learned through books but often require a teacher.

## Practicing a Profession
- **Description:** Professions like blacksmithing, brewing, and tailoring allow characters to earn gold pieces (gp) based on their lifestyle choice.
- **Time Required:** Daily or weekly activities that yield gp over time. The exact amount depends on the profession chosen.
- **Cost:** Minimal if any; primarily involves paying for materials or equipment needed for daily work.

## Recuperating
- **Description:** Characters can recover from disease, poison, and exhaustion through rest and care.
- **Time Required:** Requires a short rest (1 hour) to recover 1 hit die. A long rest (8 hours) is required for full recovery.
- **Cost:** Minimal; typically involves paying for food and lodging during the rest period.

## Researching
- **Description:** Researching involves gathering information from libraries or conducting fieldwork.
- **Time Required:** Can take days to weeks depending on the complexity of the research.
- **DC (if any):** DC 10 for simple tasks, higher for more complex research. Rewards include new knowledge and possibly magical items.

## Buying Magic Items
- **Description:** Purchasing magic items requires finding a seller who has the item in stock or negotiating with a merchant.
- **Time Required:** Varies; can be as quick as a few minutes to several days for rare items.
- **Cost:** Costs are often high, and multipliers apply based on rarity. Rare items may require additional negotiation.

## Selling Magic Items
- **Description:** Selling magic items involves finding buyers who are interested in the item or negotiating with merchants.
- **Time Required:** Varies; can be as quick as a few minutes to several days for rare items.
- **Cost:** Costs include any fees charged by sellers. Rewards include gold pieces and possibly new contacts.

## Building a Stronghold
- **Description:** Constructing a stronghold involves building a keep, tower, temple, or establishment.
- **Time Required:** Varies; can take months to years depending on the size and complexity of the structure.
- **Cost:** Costs are significant, often requiring large sums of gp. Availability checks determine if materials are readily available.

## Carousing
- **Description:** Spending gold pieces to gain contacts or create complications.
- **Time Required:** Varies; can be as quick as a few hours to several days depending on the amount spent and the desired outcome.
- **Cost:** Costs vary widely but generally require spending gp. Rewards include new contacts, information, or complications.
