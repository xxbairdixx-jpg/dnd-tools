# D&D 5e Travel and Exploration Rules

## Travel Pace
- **Fast:** 40 miles per day (12 hours of travel, +5 to Stealth checks)
- **Normal:** 30 miles per day (8 hours of travel, no bonuses or penalties)
- **Slow:** 20 miles per day (6 hours of travel, -5 to Stealth checks)

## Marching Order
- **Dungeon:** Group together for safety; use torches and light sources. Avoid drawing attention.
- **Wilderness:** Spread out to cover more ground but be cautious of traps and hazards. Use natural formations as cover.

## Travel Activities
- **Navigating:** Requires a proficiency in Navigation or the ability to read maps. DC 10 for simple terrain, higher for complex areas.
- **Foraging:** Gathering food and resources from the environment. DC 15 for common items, +2 per day if no success.
- **Tracking:** Following tracks and signs of other creatures. DC 10 for fresh tracks, -5 for old ones.
- **Mapping:** Creating detailed maps of explored areas. DC 15 for simple terrain, higher for complex areas.
- **Stealth:** Avoiding detection by enemies or non-hostile creatures. DC varies based on the situation and noise level.

## Forced March Rules
- **CON Saves:** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 + number of days forced marching) to avoid exhaustion.
- **Exhaustion:** Failure results in one level of exhaustion; success reduces it by half. Success allows travel at the normal pace for a day before needing rest.

## Wilderness Survival
- **Food and Water:** Characters need 1 meal per day (300 calories) and 2 pints of water. Failure to obtain food or water results in one level of exhaustion per day without it.
- **Shelter Requirements:** Requires finding shelter from the elements; failure results in one level of exhaustion per night spent outside.

## Weather Effects
- **Extreme Cold (DC15):** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw to avoid freezing. Failure results in 1d6 cold damage and one level of exhaustion.
- **Heat (DC10):** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw to avoid heatstroke. Failure results in 2d4 poison damage and one level of exhaustion.
- **Strong Wind (DC15):** Difficult terrain, -5 penalty to movement speed. Failure to make a Dexterity saving throw (DC 15) results in being knocked prone.
- **Heavy Precipitation (DC10):** Difficult terrain, -2 to movement speed. Failure to make a Strength saving throw (DC 10) results in one level of exhaustion.

## Random Encounter Tables by Terrain
- **Forest:** d6: 1-3: Animal, 4-5: Bandits, 6: Natural Hazard
- **Mountain:** d6: 1-2: Cave In, 3-4: Bandits, 5-6: Monster
- **Swamp:** d6: 1-2: Mire, 3-4: Monster, 5-6: Poison Gas
- **Desert:** d6: 1-3: Sandstorm, 4-5: Bandits, 6: Oasis
- **Arctic:** d6: 1-2: Ice Cave, 3-4: Frostbite, 5-6: Monster
- **Coastal:** d6: 1-3: Fishermen, 4-5: Pirate, 6: Storm
- **Grassland:** d6: 1-3: Herders, 4-5: Bandits, 6: Natural Hazard
- **Underdark:** d6: 1-2: Goblins, 3-4: Ogres, 5-6: Ancient Ruins

## Resting in the Wild
- **Short Rest (1 hour):** Requires finding a safe location. Characters can recover 1 hit die and gain advantage on next Stealth check.
- **Long Rest (8 hours):** Requires a place of safety away from danger. Characters fully recover hit points, regain spell slots, and gain temporary hit points if resting in an area with no enemies within 20 feet.

## Vehicles and Mounts
- **Speeds:** Horses (30 mph), Carriages (15 mph), Boats (10 mph)
- **Carrying Capacity:** Horses can carry up to half their weight, carriages can carry twice the weight of the horses pulling them, boats vary by type.
- **Travel Time Calculations:** Use the vehicle's speed and carrying capacity to determine travel time. Adjust for terrain difficulty as needed.

## Swimming, Climbing, Jumping Rules
- **Swimming:** DC 10 to swim without being pulled under; +2 per point of Strength modifier.
- **Climbing:** DC varies based on the surface (DC 15 for rock, DC 20 for smooth surfaces). Characters can climb up to their speed in a round if they have the Athletics proficiency or a climbing harness.
- **Jumping:** DC 10 to jump without falling; +2 per point of Dexterity modifier. Characters can jump up to twice their height and half that distance horizontally.

## Suffocation, Starvation, Dehydration Rules
- **Suffocation (DC15):** Characters must make a Constitution saving throw each round they are unable to breathe. Failure results in 1d4 poison damage per failed save.
- **Starvation (DC10):** Characters must find food or risk gaining one level of exhaustion per day without it.
- **Dehydration (DC15):** Characters must find water or risk gaining one level of exhaustion per day without it.

## Vision and Light
- **Darkvision:** Characters with darkvision can see in dim light within 60 feet. Dim light is equivalent to torchlight, bright light is like sunlight.
- **Dim Light (10 ft radius):** Reduces vision to 30 feet; characters must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 15) each round to avoid becoming disoriented and losing their way.
