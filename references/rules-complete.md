## 1. Ability Scores & Modifiers

Six abilities define every creature: **Strength (STR)**, **Dexterity (DEX)**, **Constitution (CON)**, **Intelligence (INT)**, **Wisdom (WIS)**, **Charisma (CHA)**.

**Modifier = (Score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down**

| 2–3   | −4       |

| 6–7   | −2       |

| 10–11 | +0       |
| 12–13 | +1       |

| 16–17 | +3       |

| 20–21 | +5       |

## 4. Advantage & Disadvantage

- **Advantage:** Roll 2d20, use the higher roll.
- **Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20, use the lower roll.
- Multiple sources of advantage/disadvantage **do not stack** — you still roll only 2d20.
- If you have both advantage and disadvantage from any sources, they cancel out and you roll normally.
- You can gain advantage/disadvantage from only one source and still benefit fully.

---

## 5. Ability Checks

When a character attempts an action with a chance of failure, the DM calls for an ability check.

**Roll: d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if applicable)**

The result is compared to a **Difficulty Class (DC)** set by the DM:

| Difficulty | DC  |
|------------|-----|
| Very Easy  | 5   |
| Easy       | 10  |
| Medium     | 15  |
| Hard       | 20  |
| Very Hard  | 25  |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 |

## 6. Saving Throws

Each ability has a saving throw. Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient in that save).

Saving throws are used to resist spells, traps, poisons, and other effects. The DC is determined by the effect (e.g., spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier).

## 7. Combat

### Initiative
At the start of combat, each creature rolls **d20 + DEX modifier** for initiative. Combat proceeds in order from highest to lowest.

### Actions in Combat
On your turn, you can take **one action** (plus movement and a possible bonus action):

| Action          | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Attack**      | Make one or more melee or ranged attacks |
| **Cast a Spell**| Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action |
| **Dash**        | Gain extra movement equal to your speed |
| **Disengage**   | Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks |
| **Dodge**       | Attacks against you have disadvantage; you have advantage on DEX saves |
| **Help**        | Give an ally advantage on their next ability check or attack against a target |
| **Hide**        | Make a DEX (Stealth) check to hide |
| **Ready**       | Prepare an action to trigger on a later event (uses your reaction) |
| **Search**      | Make a WIS (Perception) or INT (Investigation) check |
| **Use an Object**| Interact with a second object (first is free) |

### Bonus Actions
Some features/spells let you take a bonus action. You can only take **one bonus action per turn**, and only if a feature grants one.

### Reactions
A reaction is taken in response to a trigger (e.g., opportunity attack, *counterspell*). You get **one reaction per round**, which refreshes at the start of your turn.

### Movement
On your turn, you can move up to your speed. You can break up movement before and after your action.

### Opportunity Attacks
When a creature you can see moves out of your reach, you can use your reaction to make a **melee attack** against them. Moving away using Disengage avoids this.

## 8. Attack Rolls

**Roll: d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)**

- **Melee attacks** use STR (or DEX for finesse weapons)
- **Ranged attacks** use DEX (or STR for thrown weapons without finesse)
- **Spell attacks** use the spellcasting ability modifier

### Critical Hits
- A **natural 20** is always a hit and deals double damage dice (roll weapon dice twice, add modifiers once)
- A **natural 1** always misses

### Cover (Attack Modifiers)
- **Half cover:** +2 AC and DEX saves
- **Three-quarters cover:** +5 AC and DEX saves
- **Total cover:** Can't be targeted directly

---

## 9. Damage & Healing

### Damage Types
Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder

### Resistance, Vulnerability, Immunity
- **Resistance:** Take half damage (round down)
- **Vulnerability:** Take double damage
- **Immunity:** Take no damage

### Temporary Hit Points
- Don't stack; you keep the highest source
- Lost before regular hit points
- Don't count as healing

### Healing
- Healing restores hit points up to the maximum
- Can't exceed max HP (unless temp HP)

## 10. Conditions

### Blinded
- Can't see; auto-fail ability checks requiring sight
- Attack rolls against you have advantage; your attack rolls have disadvantage

### Charmed
- Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful abilities
- The charmer has advantage on social interaction checks with you

### Deafened
- Can't hear; auto-fail ability checks requiring hearing

### Frightened
- Disadvantage on ability checks and attacks while you can see the source
- Can't willingly move closer to the source

### Grappled
- Speed becomes 0; doesn't end other movement
- Ends if the grappler is incapacitated or you're removed from reach

### Incapacitated
- Can't take actions or reactions

### Invisible
- Impossible to see without magic; effectively heavily obscured
- Advantage on attack rolls; attacks against you have disadvantage

### Paralyzed
- Incapacitated; can't move or speak
- Auto-fail STR and DEX saves
- Attacks have advantage; melee hits are critical

### Petrified
- Transformed into solid stone (or similar); weight increases 10x
- Incapacitated, can't move/speak, unaware
- Resistance to all damage; immune to poison/disease (but don't cure existing)

### Poisoned
- Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks

### Prone
- Can only crawl (1 foot costs 2 feet of movement)
- Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Melee attacks have advantage; ranged attacks have disadvantage

### Restrained
- Speed 0; attacks have disadvantage
- Attacks against you have advantage; disadvantage on DEX saves

### Stunned
- Incapacitated; can't move; can only speak falteringly
- Auto-fail STR and DEX saves
- Attacks against you have advantage

### Unconscious
- Incapacitated; drop what you're holding; fall prone
- Auto-fail STR and DEX saves
- Attacks have advantage; melee hits are critical

## 11. Rest

### Short Rest (1 hour)
- Spend Hit Dice to heal (roll HD + CON modifier per die)
- Some class features recharge

### Long Rest (8 hours)
- Regain all hit points
- Regain up to half your total Hit Dice (minimum 1)
- Spell slots and most class features reset
- Must have at least 1 hit point to benefit
- Can't benefit from more than one long rest per 24 hours
- Interrupting a long rest (1+ hour of fighting, walking, etc.) prevents gaining benefits

---

## 12. Death Saving Throws

When reduced to 0 HP, you're **dying**:

- At the start of your turn, roll a **d20** (no modifiers):
  - **10 or higher:** Success
  - **9 or lower:** Failure
- **3 successes:** You become stable (0 HP, unconscious)
- **3 failures:** You die
- **Natural 20:** Regain 1 HP and become conscious
- **Natural 1:** Counts as 2 failures
- Taking damage while at 0 HP: 1 automatic death save failure (crit = 2 failures)
- Healing: Gain HP and reset death saves to 0/0
- Stabilized: Regain 1 HP after 1d4 hours

## 13. Exhaustion

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1     | Disadvantage on ability checks |
| 2     | Speed halved |
| 3     | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4     | Hit point maximum halved |
| 5     | Speed reduced to 0 |
| 6     | Death |

- Finishing a long rest removes 1 level (if you've eaten and drunk)
- Gaining a new level while at 6 = death

## 14. Spellcasting

### Spell Slots
- Spells of 1st level and higher require spell slots
- Casting a spell expends a slot of the spell's level or higher
- Cantrips can be cast at will without slots

### Spellcasting Ability
| Class     | Ability |
|-----------|---------|
| Bard      | CHA     |
| Cleric    | WIS     |
| Druid     | WIS     |
| Paladin   | CHA     |
| Ranger    | WIS     |
| Sorcerer  | CHA     |
| Warlock   | CHA     |
| Wizard    | INT     |

### Spell Save DC
**DC = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier**

### Spell Attack Modifier
**Attack = Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier**

### Components
- **V (Verbal):** Must speak clearly
- **S (Somatic):** Must have a free hand
- **M (Material):** Must have the component or a spellcasting focus

### Concentration
- Some spells require concentration
- You can only concentrate on one spell at a time
- Casting another concentration spell ends the current one
- Taking damage: Make a CON save (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher)
- Being incapacitated or killed ends concentration

### Ritual Casting
- Some spells can be cast as a ritual (10 minutes extra) without expending a slot
- Must have the Ritual Casting class feature

## 16. Movement

### Speed
Your speed determines how far you can move on your turn. Different movement types:
- **Walking** (default)
- **Climbing** (costs 2 feet per 1 foot unless you have a climb speed)
- **Swimming** (costs 2 feet per 1 foot unless you have a swim speed)
- **Flying** (requires a fly speed)
- **Burrowing** (requires a burrow speed)

### Difficult Terrain
Moving through difficult terrain costs **2 feet for every 1 foot** moved.

### Forced Movement
Pushes, pulls, and falls don't provoke opportunity attacks.

## 17. Vision & Light

### Light Levels
- **Bright Light:** Normal vision
- **Dim Light:** Lightly obscured — disadvantage on WIS (Perception) checks relying on sight
- **Darkness:** Heavily obscured — effectively blinded

### Darkvision
- See in dim light within range as if bright light
- See in darkness within range as if dim light (grayscale)

### Other Vision
- **Blindsight:** Perceive surroundings without sight (within radius)
- **Truesight:** See in normal and magical darkness, see invisible creatures, see through illusions, see into the Ethereal Plane

---

## 18. Encumbrance

### Carrying Capacity
**STR score × 15 lbs** (a STR 10 creature can carry 150 lbs)

### Push, Drag, or Lift
**STR score × 30 lbs**

### Variant: Encumbrance
- **Encumbered** (over 5× STR): Speed −10 ft
- **Heavily Encumbered** (over 10× STR): Speed −20 ft, disadvantage on STR/DEX/CON checks, attacks, and saves
