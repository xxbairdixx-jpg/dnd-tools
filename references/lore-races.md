# Lore of Races

## Humans
Humans are the most common race in many D&D worlds. They are versatile, adaptable, and highly resilient.

### Culture
- **Nomadic Tribes**: Live in small groups with a strong sense of community. Nomads often follow herds or trade caravans.
- **Settled Villages**: Found in towns and cities, they value stability and tradition. They are often skilled artisans and merchants.
- **Warrior Clans**: Known for their martial prowess, these clans place great importance on honor and battle.

### Naming Conventions
- Common names: John, Mary, James, Elizabeth
- Surnames: Often derived from family heritage or occupation (Smith, Miller, Archer)

### Traits Summary
- +2 to one ability score of your choice (usually Dexterity or Constitution)
- Proficient with all simple and martial weapons
- Proficient with light armor
- Can pick up a new language at no cost

## Elves
Elves are known for their longevity, magic, and connection to nature.

### High Elves
- **Culture**: Highly refined and cultured. They value knowledge, beauty, and the arts. Live in grand cities like Evermeet or Alorim.
- **Naming Conventions**: Use traditional elven names with a focus on nobility and heritage (Aldria, Eilistraee).
- **Traits Summary**: +2 to Intelligence and Charisma
- **Subclasses**:
  - **College of Lore**: Focuses on magical knowledge and spellcasting.
  - **Order of the Gauntlet**: Emphasizes martial training and sword mastery.
  - **Circle of Dreams**: Specializes in divination and dreamscapes.

### Wood Elves
- **Culture**: Live in harmony with nature, often in dense forests. They value freedom and self-reliance. Known for their archery skills.
- **Naming Conventions**: Names often reflect natural elements (Oakheart, Moonshadow).
- **Traits Summary**: +2 to Dexterity and Wisdom
- **Subclasses**:
  - **Woodland Stride**: Enhances movement through forests and woodland terrain.
  - **Favored by the Ancients**: Focuses on druidic magic and nature connection.
  - **Ranger of the Wild**: Specializes in tracking, survival, and wilderness navigation.

### Drow
- **Culture**: Known as the Dark Elves. They live underground in cities like Menzoberranzan. Highly militaristic and matriarchal. 
- **Naming Conventions**: Use dark elven names with a focus on nobility (Lirandil, Sarenrae).
- **Traits Summary**: +2 to Dexterity and Charisma
- **Subclasses**:
  - **Dread Necromancer**: Focuses on dark magic and necromancy.
  - **Shadowdancer**: Emphasizes stealth and assassination.
  - **Champion of Lolth**: Specializes in martial prowess and divine magic.

## Dwarves
Dwarves are known for their strength, craftsmanship, and deep connections to the earth.

### Hill Dwarves
- **Culture**: Live above ground in mountainous regions. They value family, honor, and tradition. Known for mining and crafting.
- **Naming Conventions**: Names often reflect ancestry or achievements (Thorin Oakenshield).
- **Traits Summary**: +2 to Constitution and Wisdom
- **Subclasses**:
  - **Draconic Warrior**: Focuses on martial prowess and dragon heritage.
  - **Stonecunning**: Specializes in mining, architecture, and geology.
  - **Artisan of the Forge**: Emphasizes crafting and metallurgy.

### Mountain Dwarves
- **Culture**: Live underground in vast caverns. They value strength, honor, and tradition. Known for their blacksmithing skills.
- **Naming Conventions**: Names often reflect ancestry or achievements (Dain Ironfoot).
- **Traits Summary**: +2 to Constitution and Strength
- **Subclasses**:
  - **Bludgeon Master**: Focuses on melee combat with heavy weapons.
  - **Stonecunning**: Specializes in mining, architecture, and geology.
  - **Artisan of the Forge**: Emphasizes crafting and metallurgy.
