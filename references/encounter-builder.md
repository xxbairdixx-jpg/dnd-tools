# Encounter Building Tools

## Encounter Difficulty Thresholds by Party Level (d20)
- Easy: 1-5
- Medium: 6-12
- Hard: 13-18
- Deadly: 19-20

## XP Budgets by Party Size and Level
- 1 PC: 1,000 XP per level
- 2 PCs: 750 XP per level
- 3 PCs: 600 XP per level
- 4 PCs: 500 XP per level
- 5+ PCs: 400 XP per level

## Monster Role Templates (d20)
- Artillery: Targets from a distance, uses ranged attacks or spells.
- Brute: Melee combatant, deals high damage with close-range attacks.
- Controller: Uses terrain and abilities to control the battlefield.
- Lurker: Hides in shadows, ambushes enemies, or strikes unexpectedly.
- Skirmisher: Fast-moving, evasive, harasses opponents from a distance.
- Soldier: Standard melee combatant, provides frontline support.

## Terrain-Based Encounter Ideas (d20)
- Forest: Ambush, Tracking, Stealth, Natural Hazards
- Mountain: Rockfall, Climbing, Precipice, Traps
- Dungeon: Puzzles, Traps, Secrets, Dark Passages
- Urban: Street Fights, Assassinations, Espionage, Marketplaces
- Aquatic: Ship Battles, Underwater Combat, Sinking Vessels, Murky Depths
- Planar: Portal Jumps, Ethereal Travel, Elemental Battles, Dimensional Rifts

## Boss Fight Mechanics (d20)
- Legendary Actions: Additional actions during combat.
- Lair Actions: Special abilities triggered by entering certain areas or triggering events.
- Mythic Traits: Unique and powerful abilities that set the boss apart from other monsters.
  - Example Trait: Aura of Doom, grants temporary hit points to allies within a radius.

## Minion and Swarm Rules (d20)
- Minions: Lesser creatures that serve as cannon fodder, typically have lower HP and weaker attacks.
- Swarms: Groups of smaller creatures, can overwhelm opponents but are less durable individually.
  - Example: Swarm of Spiders, Swarm of Goblins

## Environmental Hazards During Combat (d20)
- Lava: Burns on contact, causes damage over time.
- Cliffs: Falling off leads to instant death or severe injuries.
- Collapsing Ceiling: Triggers traps or sudden collapses.
- Poison Gas: Inhaling it deals ongoing damage and status effects.

## Chase Scene Rules (d20)
- Escape Mechanics: Opportunities for the pursued party to gain an advantage, such as taking shortcuts or using stealth.
- Complications: Obstacles that hinder escape, such as narrow paths, slippery surfaces, or terrain features.
  - Example: Narrow Canyon, Slippery Ice Path

## Social Encounter Structure (d20)
- Influence Checks: NPCs can influence the party's decisions with persuasion skills.
- Attitudes: Positive, Neutral, Negative, and their effects on interactions.
- DCs: Difficulty Class for successful social interactions, such as negotiations or combat.
  - Example DC: Diplomacy (DC15) to convince a merchant to lower prices

## Rest Interruption Chances by Environment Danger Level (d20)
- Low-Danger: 10% chance of interruption per hour
- Medium-Danger: 30% chance of interruption per hour
- High-Danger: 70% chance of interruption per hour
- Extreme-Danger: 95% chance of interruption per hour
