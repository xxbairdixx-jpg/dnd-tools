# Session Preparation Checklist and Toolkit

## Pre-Session Checklist (d20)
1. Recap: Review the last session's events, key plot points, and player decisions.
2. Hooks: Develop compelling hooks to introduce new quests or storylines.
3. NPCs: Prepare detailed character backstories and motivations for important NPCs.
4. Maps: Ensure all necessary maps are prepared, including any custom ones needed.
5. Handouts: Create handouts such as maps, charts, or character sheets if required.

## Session Pacing Guide (d20)
- Opening: Introduce the session with a brief recap and setting the scene.
- Rising Action: Develop the main storyline and key events of the session.
- Climax: Build tension and create a moment of high stakes or conflict.
- Cliffhanger: End on an exciting note that leaves players wanting more.

## Improv Techniques for DMs (d20)
1. Yes-and: Say yes to player suggestions, then build upon them.
2. Callbacks: Reference previous events and NPC actions to create continuity.
3. Raising Stakes: Gradually increase the difficulty of challenges or threats.

## NPC Voice Tips (5 Simple Voice Tricks, d20)
1. Pitch Shift: Adjust your voice's pitch for different characters or emotions.
2. Tone Modulation: Vary the tone to reflect character traits and situations.
3. Speed Control: Change speaking speed for urgency or calmness.
4. Accent Emphasis: Use accents to distinguish between different cultures or backgrounds.
5. Volume Variation: Adjust volume for emphasis, distance, or intimacy.

## Combat Encounter Flow (d20)
1. Initiative: Determine the order in which characters and monsters act.
2. Conditions Tracking: Keep track of conditions such as blindness, paralysis, or temporary hit points.
3. Morale: Monitor party morale to determine when they might flee or surrender.

## Player Engagement Tips (d20)
1. Spotlight Sharing: Ensure all players get a chance to shine and contribute.
2. Callbacks: Use references from previous sessions to create connections.
3. Consequences: Implement meaningful consequences for player actions and decisions.

## Post-Session Checklist (d20)
1. XP Distribution: Award experience points based on the session's challenges and achievements.
2. Loot: Document any treasure or items acquired during the session.
3. Loose Ends: Address any unresolved plot points that need to be addressed in future sessions.
4. Next Session Notes: Note down ideas for upcoming sessions, including potential hooks and storylines.

## Campaign Arc Structure (d20)
1. 3-Act Structure: Setup, Confrontation, Resolution.
2. 5-Room Dungeon: A series of interconnected rooms with increasing challenges.
3. Hex Crawl: Exploring a map divided into hexes for exploration and discovery.

## Difficulty Adjustment on the Fly (d20)
1. Fudging: Adjust rolls to ensure fair but exciting encounters.
2. Reinforcements: Add or remove NPCs or monsters based on player performance.
3. Terrain Changes: Modify environmental factors to affect combat dynamics.

## Common DM Rulings Quick Reference (d20)
- Grappling: How to resolve grappling rules and interactions.
- Hiding: Guidelines for successful hiding attempts, including cover and concealment.
- Surprise: Determining when a surprise round occurs and its effects on the encounter.
- Cover: Rules for determining partial or full cover in combat.
